GST (Goods and Services Tax) in India is a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax that is levied on every value addition.
Under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, an individual or HUF can claim a deduction of up to Rs. 1.5 lakh from their total income.
Income tax slabs for FY 2024-25 (Assessment Year 2025-26) under the New Tax Regime:
- Up to Rs 3 lakh: Nil
- Rs 3 lakh to Rs 6 lakh: 5%
- Rs 6 lakh to Rs 9 lakh: 10%
- Rs 9 lakh to Rs 12 lakh: 15%
- Rs 12 lakh to Rs 15 lakh: 20%
- Above Rs 15 lakh: 30%
Standard Deduction for salaried individuals is Rs 75,000 in the New Tax Regime.
GST Registration is mandatory for businesses with an annual turnover exceeding Rs 40 lakh (Rs 20 lakh for North-Eastern and hilly states).
Input Tax Credit (ITC) allows businesses to reduce the tax they have already paid on inputs from the tax they have to pay on outputs.
Section 80D allows deduction for medical insurance premium paid for self, spouse, children, and parents.
Capital Gains Tax: Long-term capital gains (LTCG) on listed equity shares exceeding Rs 1.25 lakh are taxed at 12.5%.
Short-term capital gains (STCG) on listed equity shares are taxed at 20%.
Corporate Tax rate for domestic companies is generally 25% or 30%, but can be 15% for new manufacturing companies under Section 115BAB.
 TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is required to be deducted by the payer at the time of making specified payments like salary, rent, professional fees, etc.
Professional Tax is a state-level tax on professions, trades, and employments, usually capped at Rs 2,500 per year.
